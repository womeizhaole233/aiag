# Song Dynasty Brocade Pattern Identification

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- You encounter a named decorative pattern (e.g., 方胜, 龟纹, 柿蒂) in Song-era architectural polychromy, tomb murals, or textiles.
- The pattern matches known geometric or stylized floral forms.
- You need to determine if it was part of the official, institutionally recognized brocade system referenced in Song texts like《营造法式》or《宋会要辑稿》.

Do not use this skill for:
- Patterns without documented names in Song or earlier literature.
- Non-geometric or non-floral motifs (e.g., narrative scenes, animals not tied to brocade nomenclature).

## How to Execute
1. **Confirm the pattern name** (e.g., "叠胜", "方胜", "龟纹") from visual or textual evidence.
2. **Check against documented brocade names** in authoritative Song or near-contemporary sources.
3. **Verify applicability** to one of the accepted object types: 建筑彩画, 墓葬装饰, or 织物纹样.
4. **Cross-reference with institutional records** to confirm official usage (e.g., court decrees, construction manuals, tribute lists).
5. **Conclude** whether the pattern belongs to the Song dynasty’s institutionalized brocade system.

If all conditions are met, the pattern carries formal decorative significance under Song regulatory and aesthetic frameworks.
